Eco Pi Brief  
Our idea will help preserve the world and encourage people to eat more healthily. 
We created our idea to help the overall healthiness of the UK. Our research show that 
obesity is becoming more of an issue, with 29% of the UK obese and 36% overweight. 
Our research shows that, globally, over 390 million children and adolescents were 
overweight in 2022, with 160 million obese. We also wanted to make our idea have an 
eco-friendly aspect to it; our research showed that deforestation, global warming, and 
plastic pollution are becoming more threatening! 

Our idea is a device that tells you, if the product is good or bad for the body and the 
environment. It will tell you if it is good or bad via a score out of ten, one being 
exceedingly bad and ten being exceedingly good.  
Consumers can use this product in supermarkets, shops or even when they get home 
after shopping. The Raspberry pi screen makes it interactive, requiring input from a user.  
The device has buttons/options for different types of food (carbohydrates, proteins, 
dairy etc.) that when clicked, branch off into different types of dairies, carbohydrate or 
other food groups. Once these buttons/options are pressed it will tell you if it is good or 
bad for the environment and the human body. It will also tell you how much nutrients 
are in the product and if there is something particularly bad in the product and the 
effects it will have.  

Our main target audience would be any child or teenager who consumes or buy food 
products or want to know what is in their food. However, anyone can use it. We found 
that this product is unique as it has a traffic light system and it is a physical device, 
instead of just an app. 

We have programmed the basics on our prototype, but there are so many more 
possibilities to be explored, such as where food containers could be recycled and how 
food can be used to make healthy, delicious meals. 
We want to make the device more accessible by having text, audio, and animation. We 
are also thinking of making the product dyslexia-friendly by making the screen cream or 
blue. We will also have a traffic light system that will clearly tell users of the product is 
good or bad. Our product is easy to use, with clear instructions and a comprehensive 
design. 

When we asked our school, both children and teachers, the outcome was incredibly 
positive. They all liked the traffic light system, and their only suggestion was to, instead 
of calories, nutrients might be more helpful. We will try and include all suggestions. 
Most said that they would like to use it to help them shop.
